Serrated tussock (Nassella trichotoma) floret

Nassella trichotoma

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Serrated tussock

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to southern South America and introduced to Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, and, very locally, into Europe (CFIA 2012).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Floret

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.0 - 2.5 mm

  • Width: 1.0 mm

  • Awn length: 25.0 - 35.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval-shaped, tapering to a pointed base

Seed Surface Texture

  • Surface glossy, covered with bumps that increase in size towards the top

Seed Colour

  • Straw yellow

Other Seed Features

  • Awn extends from the flat top of the floret.
  • Base is surrounded by white bristles.

Habitat and Crop Association

Pastures, grasslands, open forests (CFIA 2012). In Australia, it has spread over millions of hectares of pastureland (CFIA 2014).

General Information

Serrated tussock is a tall, tussock-forming plant that out-competes native species and reduces biodiversity (Carr et al. 1992). The species spreads by seeds that are shed from the panicles as they tumble along the ground and then remain viable in the soil (Westbrooks and Cross 1993). The majority of the seeds are produced from self-pollinating florets, creating clones of the parent plant (Osmond et al. 2008).

Similar species: Uruguayan rice grass (Piptochaetium montevidense) florets

Similar species: Uruguayan rice grass (Piptochaetium montevidense) floret

Similar Species

Uruguayan rice grass (Piptochaetium montevidense )

Florets are a similar size, lance-shape, bumpy surface texture as serrated tussock.

Uruguayan rice grass florets are a dark brown colour and have strong ridges on the surface, unlike serrated tussock florets.
