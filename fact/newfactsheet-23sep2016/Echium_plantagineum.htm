Paterson's curse (Echium plantagineum) nutlets

Paterson's curse (Echium plantagineum) nutlet, side view

Paterson's curse (Echium plantagineum) nutlet, outer side

Echium plantagineum

Family

Boraginaceae

Common Name

Paterson's curse

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian:There have been several records from Canada but it has not persisted (CFIA 2014).

Worldwide: Broad circum-Mediterranean native distribution. Introduced to Australia, South Africa, southern South America, and the western United States (CFIA 2014).

Duration of Life Cycle

Winter annual or biennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Nutlet

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.5 - 3.0 mm

  • Width: 1.5 - 2.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Nutlet is ovate with a flat base and narrow top; slightly three-angled

Seed Surface Texture

  • Densely covered in small bumps and ridges

Seed Colour

  • Colour is off-white to straw-yellow, surface between the bumps is pale.

Other Seed Features

  • Nutlet has a flat base and a pronounced ridge around the centre of the fruit.

Habitat and Crop Association

Pastures, roadsides and disturbed areas (CFIA 2012). Carries over as a weed in crops when pastures are rotated with cereals or other crops (CABI 2016).

General Information

Paterson's curse was introduced into Australia in the 1880s, where it proved to be an aggressive, drought-tolerant, herbicide-resistant and toxic weed (Kloot 1982). The plants contain toxic chemicals that can cause liver damage in pigs and horses (Culvenor 1956).

Similar Species

Blueweed (Echium vulgare):

The 2 species are similar in size, shape, colour and surface ornamentation.

Blueweed nutlets are a darker straw-yellow than Paterson's curse, and the surface ornamentation is usually low ridges and streaks rather than prominent warts.

The surface colour of blueweed (between the warts) is dark brown or black, the surface colour of Paterson's curse is pale.
