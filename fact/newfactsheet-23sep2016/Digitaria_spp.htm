Digitaria spp.

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Crabgrass

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 4 and Noxious, Class 5 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: The genus includes 3 species found in Canada, distributed across Canada with the exception of NL and the territories (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: The genus contains about 250 species originating from tropical and warm temperate parts of the world (Mabberley 2008). It includes cultivated and weedy species, some widely introduced around the world (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Most species annual; some perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Spikelet

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

Large crabgrass (Digitaria sanguinalis)

  • Length: 3.0 mm
  • Width: 1.0 - 1.3 mm

Smooth crabgrass (Digitaria ischaemum)

  • Length: 2.5 mm
  • Width: 1.3 mm

Seed Shape

  • Elongate oval, pointed at both ends

Seed Surface Texture

  • Minutely punctate in longitudinal lines, outer covering is papery and hairy

Seed Colour

  • Brown or greenish-brown, some species are black, outer covering is straw-yellow

Other Seed Features

  • The outer covering is unequal; the 2nd glume is less than half the length of the lemma.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, gardens, lawns, orchards, vineyards, shores, roadsides, railway lines and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, CABI 2016). Weedy species infest a variety of crops worldwide and are problematic in lawns (CABI 2016).

General Information

The three species occurring in Canada are: large crabgrass (Digitaria sanguinalis), smooth crabgrass (D. ischaemum) and fall crabgrass (D. cognata) (Darbyshire 2003, Brouillet et al. 2010+). The first two have been introduced into Canada and the third is native (Darbyshire 2003).

Crabgrasses are problem weeds in sandy and loam soils (Darbyshire 2003, CABI 2016). Large crabgrass plants can produce approximately 150,000 seeds in a growing season and mature plants spread over large areas by tillering soon after establishment (CABI 2016).

Similar species: Witch grass (Panicum capillare ) florets and spikelet

Similar species: Witch grass (Panicum capillare) floret, lemma side

Similar Species

Witch grass (Panicum capillare)

Witch grass florets are a similar elongate oval shape and a brown colour as crabgrass florets. The spikelets have similar straw yellow, papery, outer coverings.

Witch grass florets are smooth and shiny while crabgrass florets are dull and stippled. The papery outer covering in witchgrass is smooth and equal length, while they are unequal and hairy in crabgrass spikelets.
