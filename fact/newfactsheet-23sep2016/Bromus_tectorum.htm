Downy brome (Bromus tectorum) florets

Bromus tectorum

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Downy brome

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, MB, NB, NS, NT, ON, QC, SK, YT (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa and temperate Eurasia with a broad distribution extending from Portugal in the west to China in the east (USDA-ARS 2016). Introduced in Greenland, Iceland, Japan, Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, and North and South America (Upadhyaya et al. 1986, USDA-ARS 2016). Widely distributed throughout North America and sometimes used as forage for livestock in spring and fall (Whitson et al. 1992, Barkworth et al. 2007, Beck 2009).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual or winter annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Floret

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Floret length: 9.0 - 12.0 mm
  • Floret width: 1.0 - 2.0 mm
  • Awn length: 10.0 - 18.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oblong with a flared top

Seed Surface Texture

  • Lemma and palea are papery with hyaline margins, smooth or hairy

Seed Colour

  • Florets are straw-yellow, may have a purplish wash

Other Seed Features

  • Palea teeth longer than 1.0 mm.
  • Floret is curved away from the palea.
  • Caryopsis is dark purple.

Habitat and Crop Association

Dry areas, sand dunes, cultivated fields, pastures, rangelands, along railway lines and roadsides, and in disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, Barkworth et al. 2007). A weed of winter cereals, alfalfa and grass fields, orchards and vineyards, pastures and grasslands (Morrow and Stahlman 1984, Upadhyaya et al 1986, CABI 2016).

In western North America it dominates large areas of sagebrush and mixed grass prairie ecosystems (Ogle et al. 2003, Barkworth et al. 2007, Beck 2009). Commonly occurs on dry, mostly alkaline, sandy to loamy soils (Peeper 1984).

General Information

Downy brome was accidently introduced to western North America with cereal crops in the late 1800s, and was deliberately seeded in some pastures for forage and spread rapidly from there (Mack 1981). It is now considered the most widespread and damaging invasive annual grass in western and midwestern grasslands (Finnerty and Klingman 1962, Rinella et al. 2013).

It has a dual role as a serious weed and important forage, and is still used for early spring forage in the intermountain and Pacific Northwest regions (Upadhyaya et al. 1986, Barkworth et al. 2007). It is a prolific seed producer and common seed contaminant (Whitson et al. 1992).

Similar Species

Barren brome (Bromus sterilis)

Barren brome has a similar linear shape with an awn of similar length, and is curved away from the palea.

Barren brome has: small palea teeth, a brown caryopsis and the lemma is 14.0-20.0 mm long.  Downy brome has long palea teeth, a purple caryopsis and the lemma is 9.0 - 12.0 mm long, shorter than barren brome.
