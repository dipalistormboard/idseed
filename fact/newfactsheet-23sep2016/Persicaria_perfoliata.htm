Persicaria perfoliata

Family

Polygonaceae

Common Name

Devil's-tail tearthumb

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Previously reported from BC but did not persist (CFIA 2012).

Worldwide: Native to eastern Asia and introduced to Turkey, the West Indies and the United States, where it is found in the northeastern states and in Oregon (USDA-ARS 2016, CFIA 2012).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene, may be in a perianth

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Achene diameter: 3.5 - 4.0 mm

  • Seed diameter: 3.0 - 3.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Spherical, slightly three-angled at the top with a white dot of tissue

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, glossy

Seed Colour

  • Achene is black, rarely fading into straw yellow
  • Seed inside is chalk-white with reddish seed coat

Other Seed Features

  • Fresh or unprocessed fruits may have a bluish-purple, thick perianth covering the fruit.
  • Perianth tissue remains at the base if perianth is removed.

Habitat and Crop Association

Riparian areas as well as a wide variety of disturbed areas, including roadsides, hedges, fields, pasture and forest edges, early successional forests, plantations, gardens and parks (CFIA 2012).

General Information

Devil’s-tail tearthumb was introduced into the eastern United States in the 1940s with contaminated nursery stock (Poindexter 2010). It prefers moist to wet soils and is known for its rapid growth as well as its ability to suppress other species by smothering or shading (CABI 2016). Large infestations of devil's-tail tearthumb hamper movement due to its dense prickles (CABI 2016).

Similar species: Haldberdleaf tearthumb (Persicaria arifolia) achenes, 2 with perianth intact (brown)

Similar species: Haldberdleaf tearthumb (Persicaria arifolia) achene, perianth removed

Similar Species

Halberdleaf tearthumb (Persicaria arifolia)

Similar size, shape, black, glossy surface and fleshy perianth to devil's-tail tearthumb 

Halberdleaf tearthumb fruit is 0.5 mm larger than devil's-tail tearthumb, flattened and slightly pointed at the top.
