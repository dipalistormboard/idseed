Nodding thistle (Carduus nutans) achenes

Carduus nutans

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Nodding thistle

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs across Canada with the exception of NT, NU, PE, YT (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, temperate Asia, and Europe. Introduced in North America, Chile, South Africa, Australia, New Zealand and beyond its native range in Europe (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Biennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 4.0 - 5.0 mm
  • Width: 1.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Elongate, tapering at the base

Seed Surface Texture

  • Glossy surface, looks varnished

Seed Colour

  • Light to dark golden brown, the base is canary yellow and a yellow band may occur below the collar

Other Seed Features

  • Both longitundinal lines and transverse waves may be seen under the smooth surface coating.
  • The style peg at the top is usually short and thick.
  • Immature achenes can have a thin white pappus 1.3 - 2.0 cm long, mature achenes do not.

Habitat and Crop Association

Old fields, pastures, rangelands, roadsides and disturbed areas (Desrochers et al. 1988, Darbyshire 2003).

General Information

Nodding thistle is believed to have arrived in eastern North America in ships' ballast, and introduced into Saskatchewan through rapeseed and dispersed along railway lines (Desrochers et al. 1988).

This species produces large numbers of seeds (11,000) per plant and may form dense stands in disturbed areas such as gravel pits, roadsides and overgrazed pasture (Desrochers et al. 1988).

Similar species: Spiny plumeless thistle (Carduus acanthoides) achene

Similar species: Spiny plumeless thistle (Carduus acanthoides) achenes

Similar Species

Spiny plumeless thistle (Carduus acanthoides)

Spiny plumeless thistle achenes can be a similar size and colour, but tend to be smaller and paler than nodding thistle achenes.  The achenes have the same elongate shape with a thick top peg, and both longitudinal and transverse lines can be seen on both species' achenes.

Spiny plumeless thistle does not have the canary yellow base of nodding thistle, and the lines on the surface stand out in relief, while the lines on the surface of nodding thistle are covered with a varnish-like coating.

Immature Nodding thistle achenes may also have transverse lines on the surface, but they will have a yellow base.
