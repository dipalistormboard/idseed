Lepidium campestre

Family

Brassicaceae

Common Name

Field peppergrass

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian:Occurs in AB, BC, NB, NF, NS, ON, PE and QC (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Native to parts of Europe and western parts of temperate Asia (USDA-ARS, 2016). Naturalized in eastern temperate Asia, Europe, Australia, New Zealand, North and South America (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual or Biennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.0 - 3.0 mm
  • Width: 1.0 - 1.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval, tapers at hilum end

Seed Surface Texture

  • Roughend with small warts

Seed Colour

  • Dark reddish-brown

Other Seed Features

  • One end is rounded and the other is narrow with yellowish tissue over the hilum.
  • A line is visible between the radicle and the cotyledons on the seed.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, gardens, old fields, meadows, open-flats, forests, rocky slopes, roadsides and disturbed areas (FNA 1993+, Darbyshire 2003).

General Information

Field peppergrass is poisonous to livestock (Darbyshire 2003).

Similar Species

Globe-pod hoary cress (Lepidium appelianum)

Globe-pod hoary cress has a similar ovoid shape, a line between the cotyledons and radicle and rough surface as field peppergrass.

Globe-pod hoary cress is up to 0.5 mm shorter than field peppergrass, reddish rather than dark reddish-brown, the hilum area is rounded and the surface is not as rough.
