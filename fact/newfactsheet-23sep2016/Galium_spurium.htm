False cleavers (Galium spurium) mericarp, cross-section

Galium spurium

Family

Rubiaceae

Common Name

False cleavers

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, MB, ON, QC and SK (Malik and Vanden Born 1988)

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe and temperate Asia and naturalized elsewhere (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Mericarp

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Diameter: average 2.0 mm (1.0 - 2.5 mm)

Seed Shape

  • Round to oval with a transverse groove on the inner side

Seed Surface Texture

  • The surface is covered in hooked bristles with narrow bases, 0.1 - 1.0 mm long; fruits are occasionally smooth

Seed Colour

  • Grey-brown with white bristles

Other Seed Features

  • The hole on the inner side of the fruit is typically a transverse oval.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, forage seed crops, field borders, prairies, gardens, coastal areas, dry open woods, roadsides and waste grounds (Darbyshire 2003, Malik and Vanden Born 1988).

General Information

This species thrives in relatively dry and sunny habitats and is intolerant of shade, however it is very rare on dry prairies that receive little rainfall. Seed dispersal occurs when contaminated straw and manure as well as used animal bedding is spread onto fields. Contaminated harvesting equipment may also disperse seeds along farm roads (Malik and Vanden Born 1988).

Similar species: Cleavers (Galium aparine); mericarps

Similar Species

Cleavers (Galium aparine)

Cleavers have a similar globose shape with a hole on the inner side, grey-brown colour and dense, white bristles.

Cleavers tend to be larger than false cleavers by up to 1.5 mm, the bristles have an inflated base, the hole tends to be rounded and deep with a wide seed stalk scar.
