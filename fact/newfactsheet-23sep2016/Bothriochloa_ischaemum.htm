Bothriochloa ischaemum

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Yellow bluestem

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to Asia and southern Europe and introduced in the Caribbean, Mexico, parts of South America, and the United States (CFIA 2014).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Spikelet

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 4.0 - 4.5 mm

  • Width: 0.75 - 1.0 mm

  • Awn length: up to 15.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Long ovate with 2 stalks from the base

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, slightly shiny, outer bract has longitudinal ridges

Seed Colour

  • Pale yellow with purple tips

Other Seed Features

  • A pair of pedicels extend 3/4 up the inner bract.
  • Pedicels may have sterile spikelets attached.
  • Copious white hairs are found around the base of the spikelet, along the sides and the sides of the pedicels.
  • Short teeth are evident along the upper sides of the outer bract.

Habitat and Crop Association

Borders of fields, pastures, rangelands, dry stony places, roadsides and waste ground. Often abundant along roads, it tends to spread outwards from roadsides and into new areas (CFIA 2012).

General Information

Yellow bluestem was introduced into the United States in the early 1900s as a forage and soil stabilizer, and plantings are continuing in marginal rangeland and roadsides (CABI 2016).

Dense stands formed by yellow bluestem tend to eliminate native species, alter the soil microbes and impact wildlife biodiversity and habitat availability (CABI 2016).

Similar Species

Australian bluestem (Bothriochloa bladhii)

Australian bluestem spikelets are similar size, shape, yellow colour and smooth surface to yellow bluestem

Australian bluestem is 4.0 mm long and 1.0 mm wide, about 0.5 mm shorter than yellow bluestem and has a reddish cast and/or purple streaks.The back of the outer bract of Australian bluestem tends to be dull and have short hairs.

Silver beardgrass (Bothriochloa laguroides)

Silver beardgrass is a similar size, shape, yellow colour and smooth surface to yellow bluestem

Silver beardgrass is 3.5 mm long and 1.5 mm wide, about 1.0 mm shorter than yellow bluestem. The teeth on the back of the bracts are much shorter, and the hairs at the tips of the pedicels are 3.0 mm longer than yellow bluestem. The back of the outer bract of Silver beardgrass is smooth, shiny and a uniform colour, in yellow bluestem it is furrowed with purple tips.
