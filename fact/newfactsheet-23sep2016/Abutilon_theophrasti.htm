Velvetleaf (Abutilon theophrasti) seeds

Velvetleaf (Abutilon theophrasti) seed

Velvetleaf (Abutilon theophrasti) seed

Abutilon theophrasti

Family

Malvaceae

Common Name

Velvetleaf

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in all provinces except NL, NT, NU and YT (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+). Prevalent in southern ON and QC (Warwick and Black 1988).

Worldwide: Native to Asia (India or China) and introduced to Europe, northern Africa and North America (CABI 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 3.0 - 3.6 mm
  • Width: 2.2 - 3.6 mm

Seed Shape

  • Heart-shaped with uneven lobes and a conspicuous notch at one end.

Seed Surface Texture

  • Dull; finely granular

Seed Colour

  • Greyish brown or brownish-black and covered with a light-coloured pubescence.

Other Seed Features

  • Flap-like covering on hilum may be present

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, field margins and fence rows, gardens, roadsides, vacant lots and other waste places. A troublesome weed, especially in corn, soybean, sorghum and cotton fields (Warwick and Black 1988).

General Information

Velvetleaf was introduced into North America in the mid-1700s as a potential fibre crop (Warwick and Black 1988).

This annual species is adapted to be a weed of cultivated land and waste places, and movement of feed grain is believed to have spread it throughout eastern Canada (Warwick and Black 1988).

Similar species: Flower-of-an-hour (Hibiscus trionum) seed

Similar species: Flower-of-an-hour (Hibiscus trionum) seeds

Similar Species

Flower-of-an-hour (Hibiscus trionum)

Flower-of-an-hour seeds are a similar heart shape, brownish colour, minutely reticulate surface and a flap covering the hilum as velvetleaf.

Flower-of-an-hour seeds are densely covered with warts made up of brown hairs and resin glands, the surface is dark brown, and the lobes of the seed are equal or subequal. Velvetleaf seeds have fewer tubercles, the surface is light greyish-brown and the lobes are unequal.
