Bladder campion ( Silene vulgaris) seeds

Bladder campion ( Silene vulgaris) seed

Bladder campion (Silene vulgaris) seed, profile

Bladder campion (Silene vulgaris subsp. vulgaris); seed, point of attachment, close-up

Silene vulgaris

Family

Caryophyllaceae

Common Name

Bladder campion

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs throughout Canada except in NT and NU (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+). Very common in the eastern provinces (Darbyshire 2003).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe and temperate Asia and introduced in North America and South America, South Africa, Japan, Australia and New Zealand (USDA-ARS 2016). In the United States, found in most states except for some in the south (USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.0 - 1.7mm
  • Width: 0.9 - 1.1mm

Seed Shape

  • Round to D-shaped, with the hilum on the flat side. The prominent pads can be seen when the seed is viewed in profile.

Seed Surface Texture

  • The surface is covered in tubercles arranged in rows

Seed Colour

  • Grey to greyish-black

Other Features

  • The hilum is flanked on each side by two long, rasied pads with rectangular tubercles on their surface.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, old fields, pastures, hay fields, gardens, shores, gravel pits, roadsides, disturbed areas (Royer and Dickinson 1999, Darbyshire 2003, eFloras 2016). Not a serious weed in cultivated fields but can become weedy in zero to minimum-till fields (Royer and Dickinson 1999).

General Information

Introduction of bladder campion into North America appears to have occurred in the late 18th or early 19th century via ship ballast or contaminated clover seed (Taylor and Keller 2007).

Similar Species

Night-flowering catchfly (Silene noctiflora)

Similar shape, size, pads near hilum as bladder campion.

Bladder campion seed tends to be greyish in colour, has two prominent pads near the hilum, and is angular in outline. Night-flowering catchfly seed may have a yellowish bloom, or is reddish in colour. The hilum is bordered by two small pads and the seed is rounded in profile.

White cockle (Silene latifolia subsp. alba)

Similar shape, size, with pads near hilum as bladder campion.

Bladder campion seed is usually greyish. The hilum is bordered by two large pads, with surface tubercles in rows and is angular in profile. White cockle seed is usually covered in a whitish bloom, has an 'open mouth' hilum, with a round, inflated shape.
