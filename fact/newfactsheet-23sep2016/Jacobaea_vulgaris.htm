Jacobaea vulgaris

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Tansy ragwort

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in BC, MB, NB, NL, NS, ON, PE, QC (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe and temperate Asia and introduced in North America, China, Australia, New Zealand, and beyond its native range in Europe (Finland, Norway, Russia) (USDA-ARS 2016). In the United States, it occurs mostly in the west and northeastern regions (Kartesz 2011).

Duration of Life Cycle

Short-lived perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.0 - 2.5 mm
  • Width: 0.6 - 1.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Cylindrical; tapered at base; top is truncate

Seed Surface Texture

  • Surface has a satin sheen with a roughened surface. The achene is ribbed, with small, short, white hairs in the grooves

Seed Colour

  • The ribs are coloured light-yellow to golden brown with white haired furrows

Other Seed Features

  • A long, white pappus sometimes present on immature fruit.
  • Achenes are dimorphic; the uncommon outer fruits are hairless.

Habitat and Crop Association

Pastures, hay fields, old fields, roadsides, railway lines and disturbed areas (Bain 1991, Darbyshire 2003). Not a known weed of crops, however a problem in pastures due to toxicity (Royer and Dickinson 1999).

General Information

Tansy ragwort is thought to have been introduced into Canada in the 1850s in ships' ballast (Bain 1991). Tansy ragwort contains alkaloids which are toxic to cattle, deer, horses and goats. A single plant can produce as many as 150,000 seeds, which can remain viable for over 20 years (Ministry of Agriculture, Food, and Fisheries 2002).

Similar Species

Common groundsel (Senecio vulgaris)

Common groundsel has a similar size, cylindrical shape, longitudinal ribs and surface hairs as tansy ragwort.

Common groundsel can be up to 0.5 mm longer and 0.5 mm narrower, a darker colour and the surface hairs are more dense than tansy ragwort.
