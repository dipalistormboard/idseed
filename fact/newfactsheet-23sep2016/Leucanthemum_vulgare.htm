Ox-eye daisy (Leucanthemum vulgare) achene

Leucanthemum vulgare

Family

Asteraceae

Synonyms

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum

Common Name

Ox-eye daisy

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 and Noxious, Class 5 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in all Canadian provinces and territories (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Native to Europe and western temperate Asia (USDA-ARS 2016). Naturalized in east and southern Africa, eastern temperate and southern tropical Asia, Australia, New Zealand, North America and parts of South America (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.5 - 3.0 mm
  • Width: 0.3 - 0.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Elongate, ribbed, tapered at bottom.

Seed Surface Texture

  • There are 10 prominent ribs with smooth interspaces

Seed Colour

  • Dark brown with light brown ribs

Other Seed Features

  • A short and blunt floral style remnant is on top of the achene.
  • The rib interspaces frequently have short, white streaks.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated land (rare), hay fields, pastures, gardens, lawns, rangeland, native grasslands, forest openings, abandoned croplands, roadways, railway embankments and disturbed areas (Clements et al. 2004, Darbyshire 2003).

General Information

Ox-eye daisy was introduced to North America as an ornamental and seed contaminant in the late 1700s. By 1800, it was well established in many areas of North America (Clements et al. 2004).

Reproduction occurs mainly through seeds but plants can also sprout from rhizomes (Clements et al. 2004). A single plant may produce several thousand seeds per season that can remain viable in the soil for 5 to 40 years (Clements et al. 2004).

Similar species: Shasta daisy (Leucanthemum X superbum) achene

Similar Species

Shasta daisy (Leucanthemum X superbum)

Shasta daisy achenes have a similar shape, dark colour between the ribs and top peg as ox-eye daisy.

Shasta daisy is up to 2 x larger than ox-eye daisy: 3.0 - 4.5 mm long and 1.0 - 1.25 mm wide.  The ribs tend to be darker than ox-eye daisy.
