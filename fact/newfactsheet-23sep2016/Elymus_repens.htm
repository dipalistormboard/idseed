Couchgrass (Elymus repens) florets

Elymus repens

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Quackgrass (Couchgrass)

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in every province and territory (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Exact native range obscure, however likely native to northern Africa, Europe, temperate Asia, northern India and Pakistan. Introduced in other parts of Africa, North America, Argentina, Chile, Australia and New Zealand (USDA-ARS 2016). Occurs throughout the United States, except for some regions in the southeast (Kartesz 2011).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Floret

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 5.0 - 10.0 mm
  • Width: 1.0 - 1.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Elongate, concave on palea side, may have a short awn

Seed Colour

  • Straw-colour

Other Seed Features

  • Surface is smooth with a shiny bump at the base of the lemma.
  • The rachilla has parallel sides, with a U-shaped sinus at the base of the rachilla.
  • The palea teeth are short and thick.
  • No hairs on the callus at the base of the lemma.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, old fields, cattle yards, river flats, roadsides, railway lines, and other disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, CABI 2016). A weed of all cultivated crops (Royer and Dickinson 1999).

General Information

Couchgrass seed production is relatively low, from 25-400 per stem (Timling 2000). Seed can remain viable in the soil for up to 5 years (Timling 2000). Couchgrass also reproduces rapidly by rhizomes, which can be spread in soil (CABI 2016).

Similar Species

Western wheatgrass (Pascopyrum smithii)

The two species are similar in size, elongate shape and straw colour.

Western wheatgrass florets have a transverse groove at the base of the lemma, 2 lines of hairs on the callus and mixed long and short teeth on the palea.  The rachilla is wedge-shaped and arises from a V-shaped sinus.  The lemma and palea may be covered in a whitish bloom.

Slender wheatgrass (Elymus trachycaulus)

The two species are similar in size and elongate shape, although slender wheatgrass appears more narrow than couchgrass.

Slender wheatgrass florets tend to be paler, purple-tinged and with a slight twist. The callus has a line of hairs at the base of the lemma. Palea teeth are fine and close together, and the rachilla is covered in long hairs.
