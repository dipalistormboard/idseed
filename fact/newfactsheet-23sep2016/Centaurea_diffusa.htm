Diffuse knapweed (Centurea diffusa) achene

Centaurea diffusa

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Diffuse knapweed

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, MB, ON, QC, SK (CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to southeastern Europe and western Asia and it has expanded beyond its native range into central and western Europe and has also been introduced into North America and Argentina (CABI 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Biennial or short-lived perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 3.0 - 5.0 mm
  • Width: 1.5 - 2.0 mm
  • Pappus length: 0.1 - 1.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oblong with rounded bottom and flat top, compressed; large notch near the base

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, dull surface texture

Seed Colour

  • Light to dark reddish-brown with a strong, pale central stripe, thin pale lines that tend to be broken in the middle and a pale area around the basal notch

Habitat and Crop Association

Fields, rangelands, grasslands, dunes, open forests, roadsides, railway lines, gravel pits, industrial sites and other disturbed areas (CABI 2016, Darbyshire 2003). A serious weed of alfalfa in its native range but mainly a weed of rangelands (CABI 2016).

General Information

Diffuse knapweed can be found over a wide range of soil and environmental conditions but cannot tolerate cultivation or excessive moisture (CABI 2016). Diffuse knapweed produces large numbers of seeds that are dispersed by wind, water, and animals. Plant stems may break off to become tumbleweeds, dispersing seeds over large distances (CABI 2016).

Similar species: Spotted knapweed (Centurea stoebe) achenes

Similar Species

Spotted knapweed (Centurea stoebe)

Spotted knapweed achenes are: a similar size, shape, pappus and dark colouring with pale stripes as diffuse knapweed

Spotted knapweed is wider, with a weaker central stripe, stronger side stripes and a narrower collar compared to diffuse knapweed. The brown colour of diffuse knapweed achenes are reddish while spotted knapweed is greenish.  The pappus is also longer by 1.0 mm in spotted knapweed.
