Halogeton glomeratus

Family

Chenopodiaceae

Common Name

Halogeton

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to central Asia (CABI 2016). Introduced and widespread in the western half of the United States (USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual or Winter annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Utricle, may be in a perianth

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.2 mm

  • Width: 1.0 mm

  • Perianth Length: 1.7 - 2.2 mm

  • Perianth Width: 1.0 - 1.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval with a protruding radicle tip; compressed

Seed Surface Texture

  • Dull surface texture

Seed Colour

  • Seed is yellow-orange, perianth is straw yellow

Other Seed Features

  • Tightly coiled embryo can be seen through fruit and seed wall.
  • The perianth of some fruits is drawn out into large wings.

Habitat and Crop Association

Overgrazed rangelands, abandoned farmlands, shrublands, roadsides, railway lines, trails and disturbed areas (CABI 2016).

General Information

Halogeton was accidentally introduced into Nevada in 1934, and believed to be initially spread by sheep farming (Blackwell et al. 1979, Pemberton 1986). A prolific seed producer, it can produce more than 100,000 seeds per plant (Pemberton 1986).

Halogeton impedes revegetation due to alteration of soil chemistry, physical structure and the microbial community (Pavek 1992). The high concentration of oxalates in the tissues have caused mass mortalities in sheep (Erickson et al. 1952).

Similar species: Russian thistle (Salsola tragus) utricles, seed (upper R) and perianth (lower R)

Similar species: Russian thistle (Salsola tragus) seed

Similar Species

Russian thistle (Salsola tragus)

Similar seed shape, both have a coiled embryo visible through the seed wall.

The seed of Russian thistle is thicker than halogeton; halogeton seed appears flat.

Russian thistle seed has a perianth of a similar shape as halogeton, but the colour is grey and it is funnel-shaped while halogeton is flat.
