Tribulus terrestris

Family

Zygophyllaceae

Common Name

Puncture vine

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in BC and ON (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Exact native range obscure, possibly native to Africa, Europe, temperate Asia and Australia (USDA-ARS 2016). Introduced in North and South America. Found throughout the United States with the exception of a few states in the eastern and northeastern regions (Kartesz 2011).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual or biennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Mericarp

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Bur length: 5.0 - 6.0 mm
  • Bur width: 3.5 - 4.0 mm
  • Seed length: 2.0 - 5.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Bur is wedge-shaped or three-angled, with several spines

Seed Surface Texture

  • Dull and woody

Seed Colour

  • Straw yellow to grey-yellow

Other Seed Features

  • Seed contained in the bur is wedge-shaped and pale yellow.
  • Largest spine is 5.0-6.0 mm long.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, gardens, orchards, vineyards, roadsides and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, CABI 2016). A weed of cereals, corn, legumes, tree crops, vegetables and ornamentals across the world (CABI 2016).

General Information

Puncture vine burs easily become attached to livestock, people, farm machinery, and tires for dispersal. The spiny burs are aligned so that at least one spine is pointing upwards when lying on the soil surface. Puncture vine may also be dispersed by water or as a contaminant in hay, straw, manure, sand, gravel, and dried fruit (CABI 2016).

One plant can produce more than 5000 burs, each containing 1-4 seeds (CABI 2016). Seeds may remain viable in the ground for up to 5 years (University of California Agriculture & Natural Resources 2006).
