Fact Sheets

Silverleaf nightshade (Solanum elaeagnifolium) seeds
Silverleaf nightshade (Solanum elaeagnifolium) seed
Silverleaf nightshade (Solanum elaeagnifolium ) dried berry with seeds

Solanum elaeagnifolium

Family

Solanaceae

Common Name

Silverleaf nightshade

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northeastern Mexico and the southwestern United States. Introduced elsewhere in North and South America and currently widespread in the U.S in all but the Great Lakes and New England regions (CFIA 2012). Also introduced to Australia, India, South Africa and around the Mediterranean basin (CFIA 2014).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 3.0 - 4.0 mm

  • Width: 2.5 - 3.5 mm

  • Thickness: 0.2 mm

Seed Shape

  • Round to oval, notched at the hilum

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth with faint wavy lines, most evident along the edges of the seed

Seed Colour

  • Medium brown

Other Seed Features

  • Hilum is a wide oval opening on narrow edge.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, rangelands, grasslands, forests, coastal areas, marshes, riverbanks, roadsides and other disturbed areas, particularly in areas of low annual rainfall (CABI 2016). Affects a variety of crops, the main ones being alfalfa, citrus, corn, cotton, sorghum and wheat (CABI 2016).

General Information

Silverleaf nightshade reproduces by both seed and root fragments. Plants produce up to 250 million seeds per hectare and the seeds can remain viable for up to 10 years (Boyd and Murray 1982). All parts of the plant, especially the fruit, are poisonous to livestock (CABI 2016).

Similar species: Horse-nettle (Solanum carolinense) seeds

Similar species: Horse-nettle (Solanum carolinense) seed

Similar Species

Horse-nettle (Solanum carolinense)

Similar round shape, colour, and smooth texture as silverleaf nightshade. 

Horse-nettle seeds are approximately 0.5 mm larger in diameter, lighter colour, narrow hilum and a faint reticulate pattern under magnification.
