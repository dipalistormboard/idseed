Long-spined sandbur (Cenchrus longispinus ) bur

Long-spined sandbur (Cenchrus longispinus ) bur

Long-spined sandbur (Cenchrus longispinus) bur, top view

Long-spined sandbur (Cenchrus longispinus) floret

Long-spined sandbur (Cenchrus longispinus) floret bracts and caryopsis

Cenchrus longispinus

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Long-spined sandbur

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian:Occurs in BC, ON, QC (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to North and South America (USDA-ARS 2016). Introduced to Europe (Belgium, Italy, Ukraine) and Australia (CABI 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Spikelet in a bur

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Bur length: 5.0 - 7.0 mm

  • Bur width: 3.0 mm

  • Spikelet length and width: 2.0 - 4.0 mm x 1.5 mm

  • Caryopsis length and width: 2.0 mm x 1.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Bur shape is oval, spikelet is teardrop-shaped, grain shape is oval

Seed Surface Texture

  • Bur surface is dull and roughened with fine hairs
  • Spikelet surface is smooth and papery with longitundinal nerves
  • Floret surface is hard and roughened

Seed Colour

  • Bur colour is straw yellow with purple-tipped spines, spikelet colour is pale yellow

Other Seed Features

  • Bur has 2 types of spines: flattened spines and multiple whorls of small, thin spines around the base.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, irrigated orchards, vineyards, beaches, roadsides, railway lines, and other disturbed areas (Boydson 1997, Darbyshire 2003). A weed of corn crops in the Great Plains region of North America (Anderson 1997).

General Information

The burs of long-spined sandbur may be transported by farm equipment, contaminated hay, and other forage crops. Floating burs may travel through irrigated crops and into irrigation ditches (California Department of Food and Agriculture 2013).

Similar species: Southern sandbur (Cenchrus echinatus) bur

Similar Species

Southern sandbur (Cenchrus echinatus)

Southern and long-spined sandbur are a similar size, shape and have similar spiny burs.

Southern sandbur burs and spikelets are shorter than long-spined sandbur by up to 2.0 mm, and the burs are an round rather than oval. The inner, flattened spines on long-spined sandbur form more than one whorl; southern sandbur has only one whorl of flat inner bristles.
