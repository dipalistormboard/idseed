Striped toadflax (Linaria repens) seeds

Striped toadflax (Linaria repens) seed

Striped toadflax (Linaria repens) seeds

Linaria repens

Family

Plantaginaceae

Common Name

Striped toadflax

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in NB, NL, NS (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+)

Worldwide:Native to Europe and introduced in North America (Hanf 1983). In the United States, it is present in the northeast (USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.5 - 2.0 mm
  • Width: 1.0 -1.3 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval-shaped

Seed Surface Texture

  • Covered in ridges that form a wavy-wrinkled pattern

Seed Colour

  • Dull brownish-black, immature seeds are greyish

Other Seed Features

  • The seed edges are rounded and granular.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, old fields, pastures, thickets, roadsides, railway lines and disturbed areas (Hanf 1983, Darbyshire 2003).

Similar Species

Broomleaf toadflax (Linaria genistifolia)

Broomleaf toadflax seeds are a similar size, dark colour, rough surface and sharp winged edges as striped toadflax.

Broomleaf toadflax seeds are shiny black, have one curved edge and sharp winged edges compared to striped toadflax.

Dalmatian toadflax (Linaria dalmatica)

Dalmatian toadflax seeds are a similar size, blackish colour, rough surface and sharp winged edges as striped toadflax.

Dalmatian toadflax seeds are shiny black, straight-edged blocky and sharp winged edges compared to striped toadflax.
