Panicum spp.

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Panic grass

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 4 and Noxious, Class 5 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: The genus includes 6 species found in Canada, distributed across Canada with the exception of NL, NU and YT (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Panicum is a large genus of 300 to 600 species originating from tropical and warm temperate parts of the world (Mabberley 2008, eFloras 2016). Its numbers are difficult to estimate because its limits are not clear (Barkworth et al. 2003). Panicum includes a number of weedy species as well as species cultivated as fodders, grains and ornamentals (Mabberley 2008).

Duration of Life Cycle

Most are annual species

Seed/Fruit Type

Spikelet or floret

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

Witch grass (Panicum capillare)

  • Length of floret: 1.5 - 1.8 mm
  • Width of floret: 0.8 - 1.0 mm

Fall and Blue Panicgrass (Panicum dichotomiflorum and P. antidotale)

  • Length of floret: 2.3 - 2.5 mm
  • Width of floret: 1.0 - 1.3 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval; plano-convex

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, shiny

Seed Colour

  • The florets of most species are greenish-brown, brown, or straw-yellow with pale ends and streaking
  • Most species have 5-7 longitudinal pale nerves on the lemma

Other Seed Features

  • The callus at the base of the floret is roughened, bow-shaped and can be straw-yellow, brown or black.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, old fields, pastures, prairies, shores, floodplains, marshes, swamps, ditches, shallow water, dunes, forest edges, forest clearings, open forests, orchards, vineyards, roadsides, railway lines and disturbed areas (Barkworth et al. 2003, Darbyshire 2003, DiTomaso and Healy 2007). Witch grass (Panicum capillare) commonly infests corn, soybeans, winter wheat and sorghum, and can also infest grasslands (Clements et al. 2004).

General Information

Of the six species occurring in Canada, four are native and two are introduced (Brouillet et al. 2010+). Most species occur as weeds of agricultural habitats (Darbyshire 2003, Clements et al. 2004). Witch grass (Panicum capillare), a native species, is the most commonly found in cultivated fields and gardens (Frankton and Mulligan 1993).

Switch grass (Panicum virgatum) and proso millet ( Panicum miliaceum) are cultivated for seed and forage, and the latter has a weedy form that is common and competitive in corn fields in Canada (Royer and Dickinson 1999, Darbyshire 2003).

Similar Species

Large crabgrass (Digitaria sanguinalis)

Large crabgrass florets are a similar elongate oval shape and a brown colour as panic grass florets. The spikelets have similar straw yellow, papery, outer coverings.

Large crabgrass florets are dull and stippled while panic grass florets are smooth and shiny. The ends of the papery outer covering are unequal and hairy in crabgrass spikelets while Panic grass has smooth and equal spikelet ends in comparison.
