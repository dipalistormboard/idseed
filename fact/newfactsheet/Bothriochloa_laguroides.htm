Silver beardgrass (Bothriochloa laguroides ) spikelet outer side, short teeth along edge

Bothriochloa laguroides

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Silver beardgrass

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to North and South America, from the central U.S. to Argentina and Chile (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Spikelet

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 3.5 - 4.0 mm

  • Width: 1.5 - 1.7 mm

  • Awn length: up to 12.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Long ovate with 2 stalks from the base

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, shiny with white hairs

Seed Colour

  • Pale yellow

Other Seed Features

  • A pair of pedicels extend 3/4 up the inner bract.
  • Pedicels may have sterile spikelets attached.
  • Copious white hairs around the base of the spikelet, along the sides and the sides of the pedicels.
  • Short teeth along the upper sides of the outer bract.

Habitat and Crop Association

Field edges, pastures, grasslands, woodlands, river bottoms, and roadsides, on well-drained soils and often on limestone (Barkworth et al. 2003). Reported as a weed in alfalfa, beans and corn in Mexico (Hanan Alipi and Mondragón Pichardo 2009).

General Information

Silver beardgrass has been used in landscaping in the United States (Barkworth et al. 2003).

Similar species: Yellow bluestem (Bothriochloa ischaemum) spikelets

Similar species: Yellow bluestem (Bothriochloa ischaemum ) spikelet, teeth along edge

Similar Species

Yellow bluestem (Bothriochloa ischaemum)

Yellow bluestem is a similar size, shape, yellow colour and smooth surface to silver beardgrass.

Yellow bluestem spikelets are 4.5 mm long and 1.0 mm wide, approximately 1.0 mm longer than silver beardgrass. The teeth on the back of yellow bluestem bracts are larger, and the hairs at the tips of the pedicels are 3.0 mm shorter than silver beardgrass.

The back of the outer bract of silver beardgrass is smooth; yellow bluestem is furrowed with purple tips.
