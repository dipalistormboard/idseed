Fact Sheets

Warty bedstraw (Galium verrucosum) schizocarp and fruits

Warty bedstraw (Galium verrucosum) fruit, inner side

Warty bedstraw (Galium verrucosum) fruit, outer side

Galium verrucosum

Family

Rubiaceae

Common Name

Warty bedstraw

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Not present in Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean (Tutin et al. 1976; Hanf 1983), naturalized in central Europe (Tutin et al. 1976) and in North America, it has been introduced in Michigan (USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Schizocarp, divided into 2 fruits

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Diameter: 2.0 - 3.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Spherical, slightly compressed

Seed Surface Texture

  • Densely covered with tubercles

Seed Colour

  • Pale green to straw yellow

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, in scrub and degraded grassland (Hanf 1983).

General Information

Warty bedstraw seed has been introduced into Britain as a contaminant in imported caged bird food (Hanson and Mason 1985). Historically, it was recorded to occur rarely in corn fields (Lindley 1835).

Similar species: Cleavers (Galium aparine) fruits

Similar species: Cleavers (Galium aparine) fruit

Similar species: Cleavers (Galium aparine) fruit, hairs with inflated bases

Similar Species

Cleavers (Galium aparine)

Cleavers are a similar size, a similar globose shape with a hole on the inner side and dense, white bristles with an inflated base that appear similar to the rough surface of warty bedstraw.

Cleavers have surface hairs that are white and translucent rather than the opaque yellowish warts of warty bedstraw. The hilum area is a deep hole rather than the depression of warty bedstraw.
