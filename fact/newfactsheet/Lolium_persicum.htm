Persian darnel (Lolium persicum) florets

Lolium persicum

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Persian darnel

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, MB, ON, QC and SK (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Native to temperate Asia and southern tropical Asia (USDA-ARS 2016). In the United States, it is widespread in the upper midwest states of Montana and North Dakota (USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Floret

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Floret length: 8.0 - 11.8 mm
  • Floret width: 1.5 - 2.8 mm
  • Awn length: 5.0 - 15.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oblong, compressed

Seed Surface Texture

  • Granular

Seed Colour

  • Straw-yellow

Other Seed Features

  • There is a thin, straight, slightly flattened awn arising from the top or upper back of the lemma.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields (especially spring cereals), gardens, railway lines, roadsides and disturbed areas (Holman et al. 2004, Darbyshire 2003).

Similar species: Annual ryegrass (Lolium multiflorum) florets

Similar species: Annual ryegrass (Lolium multiflorum) floret, palea view

Similar species: Annual ryegrass (Lolium multiflorum) floret, lemma view

Similar Species

Annual ryegrass (Lolium multiflorum)

Annual ryegrass has a similar lanceolate shape, straw-yellow colour, thin awn and flattened rachilla as Persian darnel.

Annual ryegrass is smaller than Persian darnel by up to 5.0 mm.
