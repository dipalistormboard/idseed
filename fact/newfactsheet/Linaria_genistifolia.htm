Fact Sheets

Broomleaf toadflax (Linaria genistifolia) seeds

Broomleaf toadflax (Linaria genistifolia) seed

Broomleaf toadflax (Linaria genistifolia) seed

Linaria genistifolia

Family

Plantaginaceae

Common Name

Broomleaf toadflax

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian:Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to central and eastern Europe and temperate Asia (USDA-ARS 2016). Introduced in France, Germany, Norway, and the United States, where it is present in Oregon, Minnesota, and some of the northeastern states (CABI 2016, DAISIE 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.3 - 1.8 mm
  • Width: 1.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Blocky with one narrow end

Seed Surface Texture

  • Covered in ridges that form a wavy-wrinkled pattern

Seed Colour

  • Black with a bluish or copper sheen, immature seeds are greyish

Other Seed Features

  • The seed edges are winged, approximately 0.2 mm wide.

Habitat and Crop Association

Pastures, roads and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003).

Similar Species

Dalmatian toadflax (Linaria dalmatica)

Dalmatian toadflax seeds are a similar size, blackish colour, rough surface and sharp winged edges as broomleaf toadflax.

The winged edges are more curved and do not have a microscopic pattern compared to broomleaf toadflax.

Striped toadflax (Linaria repens)

Striped toadflax seeds are a similar size, dark colour, rough surface and winged edges as broomleaf toadflax.

Striped toadflax seeds have curved edges, a dull brownish surface, wrinkled flat faces and granular edges compared to broomleaf toadflax.
