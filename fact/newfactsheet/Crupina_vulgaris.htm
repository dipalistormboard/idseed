Fact Sheets

Common crupina (Crupina vulgaris) achenes
Common crupina (Crupina vulgaris ) achene
Common crupina (Crupina vulgaris ) top of the achene

Crupina vulgaris

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Common crupina

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe and western Asia. Introduced to the western United States and Massachusetts (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Winter annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 3.5 - 4.5 mm

  • Width: 2.5 - 3.0 mm

  • Pappus length: 4.0 - 7.0 mm (mostly 7.0 mm)

Seed Shape

  • Wedge-shaped with a rounded base

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, with dense hairs that give the appearance of a velvet coating

Seed Colour

  • Medium brown, with dark brown base and pappus. Often pale hairs around top

Other Seed Features

  • Persistent pappus of stiff bristles that surrounds a peg at the top of the fruit with several short spines.

Habitat and Crop Association

Pastures, rangelands, grasslands, hayfields, open woodlands, orchards, vineyards, roadsides, railroads and waste areas. Rarely found in cultivated crops, but may be found in field margins (CFIA 2012).

General Information

Common crupina was first discovered in Idaho in 1969 and had infested 9300 hectares by 1981. It was later encountered in the western states of Washington, Oregon and California, where it has since invaded over 26,000 hectares (Thill et al. 1999).

This species prefers well-drained soils and does not tolerate disturbance. Each plant produces on average 23 seeds in a season, leading to a seed production of 1000 seeds per square metre for a typical population (Thill et al. 1999).

Similar species: Southern crupina (Crupina crupinastrum ) achenes

Similar species: Southern crupina (Crupina crupinastrum ) achene

Similar Species

Southern crupina (Crupina crupinastrum)

Similar length, shape, dark brown colour and distinctive long bristle pappus as common crupina.

Southern crupina is ~ 2.0 mm wide, nearly half the width of common crupina, with a paler pappus.
