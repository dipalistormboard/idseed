Jointed goatgrass (Aegilops cylindrica) spikelet segments and caryopsis

Aegilops cylindrica

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Jointed goatgrass

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Limited populations are under official control.

Worldwide: Native to southeastern Europe and western Asia. Introduced and widespread in the United States, particularly in the western states (CFIA 2012).

Duration of Life Cycle

Winter Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Shed as a spikelet embedded in a rachis segment. Each spikelet contains an average of 2 viable seeds (Donald and Ogg, 1991).

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 8.0 - 10.0 mm

  • Width: 2.0 - 3.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Spikelet segment is straight-sided with flat ends

Seed Surface Texture

  • Surface is smooth with ridges and may have scattered short hairs

Seed Colour

  • Green when immature, golden- yellow when mature, some spikelets also have a purplish tinge

Other Seed Features

  • Most spikelets are embedded in a stem segment that floats in water.

  • Lower spikelets may have awns 3.0 mm - 9.0 mm long.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures and disturbed areas along fences, ditches and roadsides. A major weed of winter wheat (CFIA 2012).

General Information

Jointed goatgrass was introduced into North America likely in the late 1800s through contaminated wheat (Donald and Ogg 1991). It persisted and spread into winter wheat-growing areas in the western and central United States (CABI 2016). This species is genetically related to wheat and can form natural hybrids within or near wheat fields (CABI 2016).

Jointed goatgrass plants can produce more than 3000 seeds per plant (CABI 2016). The stem breaks into spikelet segments during harvesting, and the seed inside remains viable for several years (CABI 2016). The similar size and shape to winter wheat make the segments of jointed goatgrass difficult to clean out and can cause yield losses of 25 - 29 % in the field (Hubbard et al 1997).

Similar species: Itchgrass (Rottboellia cochinchinensis ) spikelet segments and caryopsis

Similar species: Itchgrass (Rottboellia cochinchinensis) caryopsis

Similar Species

Itchgrass (Rottboellia cochinchinensis)

Itchgrass has similar cylindrical spikelet segments as jointed goatgrass.

The spikelet segments of itchgrass are shorter and wider than jointed goatgrass, and lacks hairs or awns.
