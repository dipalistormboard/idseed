Medusahead rye (Taeniatherum caput-medusae) florets

Medusahead rye (Taeniatherum caput-medusae) floret, palea teeth

Medusahead rye (Taeniatherum caput-medusae) floret, palea side

Medusahead rye (Taeniatherum caput-medusae) florets

Medusahead rye (Taeniatherum caput-medusae) florets, with dark stripe down the lemma

Taeniatherum caput-medusae

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Medusahead rye

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe and western and middle Asia. Introduced to Australia, Chile and the United States (USDA-ARS 2016). In the U.S., it is found in the western states and occasionally at other sites in the east, where it may not persist (Barkworth et al. 2007).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Floret

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 7.0 - 10.0 mm

  • Width: 1.75 mm

  • Awn length range: 30 - 110 mm (often broken)

Seed Shape

  • Elongate, tapering at the top

Seed Surface Texture

  • Hard, shiny surface covered in small bumps with teeth along sides

Seed Colour

  • Straw-yellow to yellow, dark stripe down the middle of the lemma

Other Seed Features

  • Awn appears as extension of lemma with teeth along edges.
  • Lemma arches backwards.

Habitat and Crop Association

Rangelands, grasslands, sagebrush communities, woodlands, roadsides and disturbed areas (CABI 2016, Barkworth et al. 2007).

General Information

Medusahead rye is believed to have been first introduced into the United States in livestock fur or contaminated cereal grain (CABI 2016).

Medusahead rye is competitive and forms dense stands in rangelands, altering their ecology (CABI 2016). Downy brome is a known associate (Bovey et al. 1960).

Similar species: Ripgut brome (Bromus diandrus); florets

Similar species: Ripgut brome (Bromus diandrus); floret, palea side

Similar Species

Ripgut brome (Bromus diandrus)

Similar elongated shape, roughened texture and flat awn as medusahead rye.

Ripgut brome lemmas are up to 10.0 mm longer than medusahead rye and have a leathery surface texture, while medusahead rye lemmas are papery.

Ripgut brome florets and grains are a reddish colour with thin palea teeth, while medusahead rye lemmas are straw-yellow with a dark stripe and stout palea teeth.
