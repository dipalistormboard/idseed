Fact Sheets

Cheat (Bromus secalinus) caryopsis (L) and florets

Cheat (Bromus secalinus), base of floret

Cheat (Bromus secalinus), base of floret

Cheat (Bromus secalinus) caryopsis

Bromus secalinus

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Cheat

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, NB, NS, ON, QC, YT (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to southern and eastern Europe, northern Africa and temperate Asia (USDA-ARS 2016). Introduced in Australia and North and South America, and common throughout most of the United States (Whitson et al. 1992, Barkworth et al. 2007, USDA-ARS 2016). Cultivated in the western U.S. (Oregon, Washington) (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Floret

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Floret length: 6.5 - 8.5 mm
  • Floret width: 1.75 - 2.5 mm
  • Awn length: 3.0 - 6.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Long, oval-shaped with extended sides.

Seed Surface Texture

  • Lemma and palea are smooth with hairy margins

Seed Colour

  • Straw yellow

Other Seed Features

  • Palea teeth thin and shorter than 1.0 mm.
  • Caryopsis thicker than other species of Bromus spp. with inrolled sides.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, old fields, grassy places, slopes, shores, railway lines, roadsides, waste ground and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, Barkworth et al. 2007, eFloras 2015). A weed of winter wheat and alfalfa fields (Davis 1993, Stone et al. 2001).

General Information

Cheat was historically a serious weed of cereal crops, perhaps accidently harvested and planted with them (Georgia 2007). Its decline in the second half of the 20th century has been attributed to improvements in seed cleaning technology (Wilson and King 2003, Adamczewski et al. 2015).

It has recently re-emerged as a pest of winter wheat in Europe, and minimum tillage practices are thought to promote this species (Adamcszewski et al. 2015). Cheat occurs as a contaminant in seed and grain (Canadian Grain Commission (CGC) data).

Similar species: Japanese brome (Bromus japonicus) florets

Similar species: Japanese brome (Bromus japonicus ) floret, flat caryopsis

Similar Species

Japanese Brome (Bromus japonicus)

Japanese brome has a similar size, shape, colour, palea teeth and extened lemma sides.

Japanese brome has a shorter and thinner awn than cheat, and the caryopsis is flat rather than curled inwardly.
