Lepidium chalepense

Family

Brassicaceae

Synonyms

Cardaria chalepensis

Common Name

Lens-pod hoary cress

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, MB, ON, SK (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to the Middle East and China and introduced in North America and Argentina (USDA-ARS 2016). In the United States it occurs in the west (Francis and Warwick 2008).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.3 - 3.0 mm long
  • Width: 1.5 mm wide

Seed Shape

  • Oval, compressed, the hilum area is flattened

Seed Surface Texture

  • Granular

Seed Colour

  • Reddish-brown

Other Seed Features

  • The pods are globose, strongly inflated, 2.5 - 6.0 mm long and 4.0 - 6.0 mm wide, usually hairy.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, old fields, grasslands, pastures, feed lots, riparian areas, ditches, roadsides, railway lines and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, Zouhar 2004, Francis and Warwick 2008). A weed of alfalfa fields and pastures in Canada (Francis and Warwick 2008).

General Information

Contaminated alfalfa seed or horse feed was the probable cause of lens-pod hoary cress seed introduction into North America (Francis and Warwick 2008).

Similar Species

Globe-pod hoary cress (Lepidium appelianum)

Globe-pod hoary cress seeds are a similar size, reddish-brown colour, rough surface and oval shape as lens-pod hoary cress.

Globe-pod hoary cress pods are smaller by approximately 1.0 mm; the seeds are an even oval-shaped in cross-section due to a larger seed root than lens-pod hoary cress.

Heart-pod hoary cress (Lepidium draba subsp. draba)

Heart-pod hoary cress seeds are a similar size, roughened surface and oval shape as lens-pod hoary cress.

Heart-pod hoary cress pods are an inverted heart shape and deflated at maturity, the seeds are darker brown with a surface net pattern and egg-shaped in cross-section with constrictions between the cotyledons and the seed root.
