Cow cockle (Vaccaria hispanica) seeds

Cow cockle (Vaccaria hispanica) seed

Cow cockle (Vaccaria hispanica) seed, cross-section

Vaccaria hispanica

Family

Caryophyllaceae

Synonyms

Saponaria vaccaria

Common Name

Cow cockle

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, MB, NB, NS, ON, QC, SK, YT (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe and temperate Asia and introduced in North America, Argentina, Uruguay, northeast Africa, South Africa, Australia, and beyond its native range in Asia (Arabian Peninsula, Japan, Thailand) (USDA-ARS 2016). Widespread in the United States (USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Diameter: 1.5 - 2.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Globose

Seed Surface Texture

  • Distinctive pattern of small tubercles arranged in rows over the surface of the seed

Seed Colour

  • Mature seeds are black; immature seeds are reddish-brown

Other Seed Features

  • Slightly flattened around the outside of the seed.
  • Hilum a small opening on the flattened edge.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, old fields, roadsides, railway lines and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003).

General Information

Cow cockle prefers fine-textured soils (Frankton and Mulligan 1993). It was a common weed of grain crops in the past, but its numbers and distribution have been declining over the last few decades (FNA 1993+). The saponins contained in the plants and seeds are potentially toxic to livestock if ingested (Frankton and Mulligan 1993).

Similar species: Bouncingbet (Saponaria officinalis) seeds

Similar Species

Bouncingbet (Saponaria officinalis)

Bouncingbet seeds are a similar size, black colour, round outline and rough surface pattern as cow cockle seeds.

Bouncingbet seeds are flattened, the hilum is in a notch, and the surface pattern is made of raised cells rather than tubercles as in cow cockle seeds.
