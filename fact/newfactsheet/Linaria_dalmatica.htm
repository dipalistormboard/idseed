Dalmatian toadflax (Linaria dalmatica ) seeds

Dalmatian toadflax (Linaria dalmatica ) seed

Dalmatian toadflax (Linaria dalmatica ) seed showing bronze highlights

Dalmatian toadflax (Linaria dalmatica ) seed

Dalmatian toadflax (Linaria dalmatica ) seed, cross-section

Linaria dalmatica

Family

Plantaginaceae

Common Name

Dalmatian toadflax

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, MB, NB, NS, ON, QC, SK and ephemeral in NL and YT (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to southeastern Europe and western Asia (USDA-ARS 2016). Introduced in North America, Argentina, South Africa, India, Australia, and beyond its native range in Europe (Germany, Hungary, Italy, Switzerland and the United Kingdom)(CABI 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.0 - 2.0 mm
  • Width: 1.0 -1.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Rectangular-blocky

Seed Surface Texture

  • Covered in ridges that form a wavy-wrinkled pattern

Seed Colour

  • Black with bronze highlights

Other Seed Features

  • The seed edges are winged, approximately 0.2 mm wide.

Habitat and Crop Association

Old fields, pastures, gardens, vineyards, forest plantations, roadsides, railway lines and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, CABI 2016). Persists in alfalfa and summerfallow in North America (Vujnovic and Wein 1997).

General Information

Dalmatian toadflax was introduced into North America in the late 19th century as an ornamental (Vujnovic and Wein 1997). It is adaptable to a wide range of environmental conditions and soil types, but is mostly found on coarse soils in sparsely vegetated sites (Vujnovic and Wein 1997).

Dalmatian toadflax is a perennial species that produces large numbers of seed every year (up to 400,000) and can also regrow from root pieces (Vujnovic and Wein 1997). This combination of characteristics makes eradication difficult; some success has been found with biological control (Vujnovic and Wein 1997).

Similar Species

Broomleaf toadflax (Linaria genistifolia)

Broomleaf toadflax seeds are a similar size, blackish colour, rough surface and sharp winged edges as Dalmatian toadflax.

Broomleaf toadflax seeds have one curved edge and the winged edges have a microscopic reticulate pattern compared to Dalmatian toadflax.

Striped toadflax (Linaria repens)

Striped toadflax seeds are a similar size, dark colour, rough surface and winged edges as Dalmatian toadflax.

Striped toadflax seeds have curved edges, a dull brownish surface, wrinkled flat faces and granular edges compared to Dalmatian toadflax.
