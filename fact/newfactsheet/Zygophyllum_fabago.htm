Fact Sheets

Syrian bean-caper (Zygophyllum fabago) seeds
Syrian bean-caper (Zygophyllum fabago) seed

Syrian bean-caper (Zygophyllum fabago) seed, side view

Syrian bean-caper (Zygophyllum fabago ) surface of seed

Zygophyllum fabago

Family

Zygophyllaceae

Common Name

Syrian bean-caper

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to eastern Europe, the Middle East and central Asia. Introduced to Australia, southern Europe and the western United States (CFIA 2012, 2014).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 4.0 mm - 5.5 mm

  • Width: 2.5 mm

  • Thickness: 1.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Ovate to D-shaped, flattened

Seed Surface Texture

  • Surface covered with a crust with many pits exposing the seed coat beneath

Seed Colour

  • Surface crust is grey to yellowish-white, seed is translucent red.

Habitat and Crop Association

Dry rangelands, grasslands and disturbed areas, including roadsides, corrals and gravel pits (CFIA 2014).

General Information

Syrian bean-caper is thought to have been imported into the United States in contaminated alfalfa seed (Davison and Wargo 2001). This species usually spreads by seed, but can also generate new plants from pieces of its roots (CFIA 2014).

Syrian bean-caper prefers dry habitats, especially rangelands, where it outcompetes native species (Davison and Wargo 2001).
