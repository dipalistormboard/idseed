Horse nettle (Solanum carolinense) seed

Solanum carolinense

Family

Solanaceae

Common Name

Horse nettle (Ball nettle)

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in ON and QC (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to eastern North America, from Ontario to northern Mexico, and introduced in Japan and India (Bassett and Munro 1986, USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.5 - 3.0 mm

  • Width: 1.5 - 2.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval shaped, flattened

Seed Surface Texture

  • Faint pattern of wavy-edged cells can be seen under magnification

Seed Colour

  • Yellow to orange

Other Seed Features

  • Hilum is a slit along the narrow edge, 0.5 mm long.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, gardens, nurseries, riverbanks, roadsides and disturbed areas (Bassett and Munro 1986, Darbyshire 2003). Most frequently found in corn and grain fields, and to a lesser extent in pastures, alfalfa, potatoes, soybeans and tomatoes (Bassett and Munro 1986).

General Information

Horse nettle may produce up to 5000 seeds per plant. The seeds are apparently spread through livestock manure and the berries and horizontal roots are spread as a result of harvesting operations (Bassett and Munro 1986, CABI 2016).

Similar species: Silverleaf nightshade (Solanum elaeagnifolium) seeds

Similar species: Silverleaf nightshade (Solanum elaeagnifolium) seed

Similar Species

Silverleaf nightshade (Solanum elaeagnifolium)

Silverleaf nightshade seeds are a similar flattened oval shape, orange colour and satiny surface as horse nettle seeds.

Silverleaf nightshade seeds tend to be browner with a wider hilum and the surface pattern tends to be more elongate, with long, wavy lines around the outside of the seed.
