Avena sterilis

Family

Poaceae

Common Name

Sterile oat

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in ON (ephemeral); previously reported from QC but currently excluded from the flora (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region; associated with the cultivation of oats (CABI 2016; USDA-ARS 2016). Widely introduced elsewhere and present on all continents except Antarctica (CABI 2016; USDA-ARS 2016). Occurs in the United States, in: California, Colorado, New Jersey, Ohio, Oregon and Pennsylvania (Kartesz 2011, USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Pair of florets

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 10.0 - 27.0 mm
  • Width: 2.5 - 4.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Elongate, tapers at the top into long, papery lemma extensions

Seed Surface Texture

  • Pebbled texture; long, stiff reddish hairs around the base of the floret

Seed Colour

  • Tends to be a reddish colour with a straw yellow tip, but can be solid straw yellow

Other Seed Features

  • Florets usually shed as a pair.
  • The callus at the base of the floret is prominent and elongated.
  • The caryopsis does not have an indentation where the awn was attached to the lemma.
  • A strongly bent and twisted awn is attached to the upper 1/2 of the lemma of the floret, 30.0 to 80.0 mm long.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, roadsides and disturbed ground. A serious weed of arable crops, particularly cereals, with a life cycle that mirrors many annual crops. Often occurs on heavier soils, sometimes replacing the similar species A. fatua in this soil type where the two co-occur (CABI 2016).

General Information

Like wild oat, sterile oat has been dispersed around the world as a contaminant in cereal grain and seed, as well as on agricultural equipment, straw, hay, livestock and wool. A crop mimic in Avena sp. (oat) crops, it produces seeds that are difficult to separate from grain (CABI 2016).

Individual plants can produce up to 200 seeds, although the average in a natural population has been reported as 13-21 seeds per plant; seeds remain viable for up to 5 years in the soil (CABI 2016).

Similar Species

Wild oat (Avena fatua)

Wild oat florets are a similar elongated shape, reddish colour, presence of callus hairs, prominent callus, and twisted awn.

Wild oat florets tend to be longer (by up to 20.0 mm), shorter callus hairs (up to 2.0 mm), longer callus, not shed in pairs and the papery lemma tips are 1.0 - 2.0 mm shorter when compared to sterile oat.  The awn can be up to 50.0 mm smaller than sterile oat.
