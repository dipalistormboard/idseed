Spiny plumeless thistle (Carduus acanthoides) achene

Carduus acanthoides

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Spiny plumeless thistle

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian:Occurs in BC, NS, ON, and QC and ephemeral in NB and NL (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, temperate Asia, and most of Europe. Introduced to North America, Argentina, Uruguay, New Zealand and beyond its native range in Europe (USDA-ARS 2016). Occurs across the continental United States, with the exception of some southwestern and southeastern states (Kartesz 2011).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual or biennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.5 - 3.0 mm
  • Width: 1.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Elongate, tapering at the base, compressed, may have a small hump to one side of the collar

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, with transverse wrinkles on the surface

Seed Colour

  • Cream-coloured to light brown, often has a pinkish tinge. The collar at the top of the achene may be yellow

Other Seed Features

  • Both longitudinal lines and transverse waves may be seen on the surface of the achene.
  • The style peg at the top is usually short and thick.
  • Immature achenes can have a filamentous white pappus 1.3 - 2.0 cm long, mature achenes do not.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, rangelands, roadsides and disturbed areas (Desrochers et al. 1988, Darbyshire 2003).

General Information

Spiny plumeless thistle was first recorded on the east coast of North America in the late 19th century, possibly arriving in ships' ballast (Desrochers et al.1988). It may form dense stands on disturbed sites and invade native and restored grasslands (Ministry of Agriculture, Food, and Fisheries 2002).

Similar species: Nodding thistle (Carduus nutans) achenes

Similar species: Nodding thistle (Carduus nutans) achene

Similar Species

Nodding thistle (Carduus nutans)

Nodding thistle achenes can be a similar size and colour, but can be larger and darker than spiny plumeless thistle achenes. The achenes have the same elongate shape with a thick top peg, and longitudinal and transverse lines can be seen on both species' achenes.

Nodding thistle achenes have a canary yellow base and the lines on the achene surface of nodding thistle achenes are covered with a smooth, varnish-like coating.

Immature nodding thistle achenes may also have transverse wrinkles on the surface, but they will have a yellow base.
