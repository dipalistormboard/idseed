Fact Sheets

South African ragwort (Senecio inaequidens) achenes

South African ragwort (Senecio inaequidens ) achene

South African ragwort (Senecio inaequidens ) achene surface

South African ragwort (Senecio inaequidens ) achene top view

Senecio inaequidens

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

South African ragwort

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to southern Africa and introduced to many European countries and Taiwan (CFIA 2014). Reports from Mexico and parts of South America may refer to Senecio madagascariensis (CFIA 2012).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.0 - 2.5 mm

  • Width: 0.2 - 0.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oblong with a narrow base

Seed Surface Texture

  • Several longitudinal ribs; surface between the ribs is roughened, appears sparkling under lights

Seed Colour

  • Usually dark brown, may be light brown or green in immature specimens

Other Seed Features

  • Surface between the ribs is densely covered with worm-like white hairs.
  • Immature achenes have a long, white, sparse pappus 2-3 times the length of the achene.

Habitat and Crop Association

Colonizes a wide range of open ruderal habitats, as well as stream banks, coastal dunes, grasslands, vineyards and pastures (CFIA 2012).

General Information

Historic introduction sites of South African ragwort in Europe were closely tied with the wool industry (CABI 2016). Pastures that contain South African ragwort produce lower quality forage due to the poisonous alkaloids in the plant (Dimande et al. 2007).

This species produces thousands of seeds per year that can be carried by water, hay, humans or machinery into new habitats (CABI 2016, Heger and Böhmer 2006 ).

Similar species: Common groundsel (Senecio vulgaris) achenes

Similar Species

Common groundsel (Senecio vulgaris)

Similar size, oblong shape, and surface hairs. 

Common groundsel tends to be sharply narrowed at both ends, South African ragwort is narrowed at one end. The surface hairs of common groundsel are short and sparse, the hairs of South African ragwort are longer and more dense.

Madagascar ragwort (Senecio madagascariensis)

Similar oblong shape, rough texture, surface hairs, and is closely related to South African ragwort.

Madagascar ragwort achenes are up to 1.0 mm shorter than South African ragwort, usually red brown with short white hairs on the ribs.
