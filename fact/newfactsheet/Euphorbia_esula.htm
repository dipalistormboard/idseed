Leafy spurge (Euphorbia esula) seed, cross-section

Euphorbia esula

Family

Euphorbiaceae

Common Name

Leafy spurge

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs across Canada except in NL, NT and NU (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to central and eastern Europe and temperate Asia and introduced in northern Europe and North America (Canada, the United States and Mexico) (USDA-ARS 2016). In the United States it occurs across northern areas and into the southwest (Kartesz 2011).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.5 - 3.0 mm
  • Width: 1.5 - 2.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval; hilum end is flattened

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, with a raphe on one side

Seed Colour

  • Surface is usually light grey, with reddish-brown mottles, but can be orange to brown.

Other Seed Features

  • The raphe extends the length of the seed, ending at the hilum.
  • The hilum is usually covered by a caruncle.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, rangelands, fields and roadsides (Royer and Dickinson 1999, Darbyshire 2003).

General Information

In the United States, leafy spurge is a particular problem in the northern and central plains states (CABI 2016). It prefers moist sites and disturbed soils and is aggressive in semi-arid situations or overgrazed pastures where competition is reduced. It can also establish in healthy natural ecosystems and in untilled, non-cropland habitats (CABI 2016).

Seed is dispersed by ejection from the capsule when ripe, up to a distance of 4.6 m. The seed may also be dispersed by water, wind, and animals. Seed in the soil may remain viable for up to 8 years (CABI 2016).

Similar Species

Cypress spurge (Euphorbia cyparissias)

The seeds of cypress spurge have a similar barrel shape, grey colour and tissue over the hilum as leafy spurge.

Cypress spurge seeds are slightly smaller than leafy spurge at 2.0 - 2.5 mm long by 1.0 - 2.0 mm wide.  Most are a solid light grey colour without brown mottles.
