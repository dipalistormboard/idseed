Fact Sheets

Squarrose knapweed (Centurea virgata subsp. squarrosa) achenes

Squarrose knapweed (Centurea virgata subsp. squarrosa) achene

Squarrose knapweed (Centurea virgata subsp. squarrosa) achene, edge view

Squarrose knapweed (Centurea virgata subsp. squarrosa) achene, basal notch

Centaurea virgata subsp. squarrosa

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Squarrose knapweed

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to temperate Asia and introduced in the western United States and Michigan (USDA-ARS 2016, DiTomaso and Healy 2007).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size:

  • Length: 2.5 - 3.5 mm

  • Width: 1.5 mm

  • Pappus length: 1.0 - 2.5 mm

Seed Shape:

  • Oblong with rounded bottom and flat top, compressed; small notch near the base

Seed Surface Texture:

  • Smooth, dull

Seed Colour:

  • Light brown to straw-coloured, strong central stripe and numerous thin side stripes

Other Seed Features

  • Collar at top of the achene is narrow and yellowish.
  • Notch at the base is shallow (under 1.0 mm depth).

Habitat and Crop Association

Degraded rangelands, grasslands, open sites, logged areas, roadsides (Cal-IPC 2016).

General Information

Squarrose knapweed was accidently introduced into California in 1930s with sheep ranching (Howell 1959). This species tolerates a wide range of temperatures and drought conditions, but does not tolerate cultivation or irrigation (Whitson et al. 1996).

Similar species:Spotted knapweed (Centurea stoebe) achene

Similar species:Spotted knapweed (Centurea stoebe) achenes

Similar Species

Spotted knapweed (Centaurea stoebe)

Spotted knapweed achenes are a similar size, shape, dark colour, striped pattern and pappus length as squarrose knapweed.

Spotted knapweed is narrower (by 0.5 mm), often lighter in colour with a more shallow notch (by 0.5 mm) and more narrow pale area around the notch than squarrose knapweed.
