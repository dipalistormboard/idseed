Plantago lanceolata

Family

Plantaginaceae

Common Name

Ribgrass

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in BC, MB, NB, NF, NS, ON, QC and PE (Brouillet et al. 2010+)

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe, temperate Asia and south tropical Asia (USDA-ARS 2016). Naturalized in eastern Asia, southern Africa, Australia, New Zealand, North America and South America (USDA-ARS 2016, CABI 2016). Widespread in the United States (USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.0 - 3.0 mm
  • Width: 1.3 mm

Seed Shape

  • Elongate oval in outline, flattened, furrowed on one side

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, with slight wrinkling

Seed Colour

  • Dark reddish-brown

Other Seed Features

  • Hilum is in a furrow on one side of the seed.
  • Other side has a lighter central stripe that follows the furrow.

Habitat and Crop Association

Clover fields, dry grasslands, old pastures, hayfields, old fields, forest openings, lawns, railway embankments, roadsides and disturbed areas, especially open dry areas (Cavers et al. 1980, Darbyshire 2003).

General Information

The first report of ribgrass in North America dates from 1829 in Nova Scotia (Cavers et al. 1980). In New Zealand, cultivars have been developed for forage yield and suitability for livestock grazing (CABI, 2016). Pollens are common allergens (Frankton and Mulligan 1993).

Similar species: Psyllium (Plantago psyllium) seeds

Similar species: Psyllium (Plantago psyllium) seed

Similar Species

Psyllium (Plantago psyllium)

Psyllium seeds are a similar size, reddish-brown colour, glossy surface, a central furrow on the hilum side, and lighter central stripe on the other as ribgrass seeds.

Psyllium seeds are smoother than ribgrass seeds, can be shorter by up to 0.5 mm, and are slightly diamond-shaped in outline compared to ribgrass seeds.
