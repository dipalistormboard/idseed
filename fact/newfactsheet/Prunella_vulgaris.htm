Fact Sheets

Heal-all (Prunella vulgaris) nutlets

Heal-all (Prunella vulgaris) nutlet

Heal-all (Prunella vulgaris) nutlet, side view

Prunella vulgaris

Family

Lamiaceae

Common Name

Heal-all

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 4 and Noxious, Class 5 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs throughout Canada except in NU and NT (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+). There are native and introduced populations (Darbyshire 2003).

Worldwide: Exact native range obscure, present throughout most of North America, Europe, northern Africa and Asia and introduced in other temperate areas such as Australia, New Zealand and southern Africa (eFloras 2016, USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Nutlet

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.8 - 2.3 mm
  • Width: 1.3 - 1.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Teardrop-shaped, flattened

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, shining

Seed Colour

  • Reddish-brown

Other Seed Features

  • Hilum at the narrow end of the seed, associated with white tissue.
  • Distinctive dark lines run down the centre of each face and around the outside.

Habitat and Crop Association

Old fields, gardens, pastures, grasslands, lawns, turf, thickets, open forests, riparian and wetland sites, roadsides, railway lines and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, DiTomaso and Healy 2007).

General Information

Many cultures have used heal-all to help heal wounds and deal with inflammation (Oh et al. 2011). This species is reported to have both antiviral and antibacterial properties (Oh et al. 2011).

Similar species: Large-flowered selfheal (Prunella grandiflora) nutlets

Similar species: Large-flowered selfheal (Prunella grandiflora) nutlet

Similar Species

Large-flowered selfheal (Prunella grandiflora)

Large-flowered selfheal nutlets are a similar size, reddish-brown colour, similar white tissue at the hilum and distinctive dark lines as heal-all.

Large-flowered selfheal nutlets are an oval shape with 2 or more dark stripes on the outside of the seed when compared to heal-all nutlets.
