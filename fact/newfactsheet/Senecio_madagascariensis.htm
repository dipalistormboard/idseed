Fact Sheets

Madagascar ragwort (Senecio madagascariensis) achenes

Madagascar ragwort (Senecio madagascariensis) achene

Madagascar ragwort (Senecio madagascariensis) achene surface

Madagascar ragwort (Senecio madagascariensis)achene, top view

Senecio madagascariensis

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Madagascar ragwort

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to southern Africa and Madagascar and introduced to Argentina, Australia, Colombia, Japan, Kenya, Mauritius, Réunion and the United States (Hawaii) (CFIA 2014).

Duration of Life Cycle

Short-lived perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.0 - 3.0 mm

  • Width: 0.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oblong with narrow ends

Seed Surface Texture

  • Several longitudinal ribs; surface between the ribs appears sparkling under lights

Seed Colour

  • Dark yellow to reddish-brown

Other Seed Features

  • Short, worm-like white hairs between the ribs.
  • Immature achenes have a long, white pappus 4.0 - 6.5 mm long.

Habitat and Crop Association

Colonizes a wide range of habitats, elevations and soil types. Often found in arid or moist pastures, coastal plains, yards, fields and roadsides (CFIA 2012).

General Information

Madagascar ragwort is a serious pest plant in Australia and Hawaii (Starr et al. 1999). This pioneer plant produces several thousands of seeds every year that are dispersed by wind or water (Starr et al. 1999). Infested pastures suffer lowered productivity through competition and toxicity to livestock (CABI 2016).

Similar Species

Common groundsel (Senecio vulgaris)

Similar size, oblong shape, and surface hairs.

Common groundsel tends to be sharply narrowed at both ends, where Madagascar ragwort is narrowed at one end.  The surface hairs are dense; Madagascar ragwort has sparse hairs.

South African ragwort (Senecio inaequidens)

Similar oblong shape, ribs, and surface hairs and is closely related to Madagascar ragwort.

South African ragwort is up to 1.0 mm longer than Madagascar ragwort, and is usually dark brown and densely covered with long white hairs.
