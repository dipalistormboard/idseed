Galium mollugo

Family

Rubiaceae

Common Name

False baby's breath

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, NB, NF, NS, ON, PE and QC (Brouillet et al. 2010+, Mersereau and DiTomasso 2003).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe and western parts of temperate Asia (USDA-ARS 2016). In North America, it is present in western and eastern sides of the continent (USDA-NRCS 2016, Mersereau and DiTommaso 2003).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Mericarp

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.0 - 2.0 mm
  • Width: 0.5 - 1.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval- shaped with longitudinal groove on inner side

Seed Surface Texture

  • Wrinkled, may also be bubbled

Seed Colour

  • Brown to dark-brown

Other Seed Features

  • The hilum is a long groove on the inner side of the fruit

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, natural meadows, old fields, forest edges, river flats, roadsides and waste places (Mersereau and DiTommaso 2003).

General Information

False baby's breath was introduced into North America as an ornamental plant, and first recorded in Canada in the late 1800s (Mersereau and DiTommaso 2002). Hybrids with the similar yellow bedstraw (Galium verum) have been recorded (Mersereau and DiTommaso 2002).

Similar Species

Northern bedstraw (Galium boreale)

Seeds of northern bedstraw are a similar size, reniform shape, brown colour and grooved hilum as smooth bedstraw.

Northern bedstraw seeds are covered in flat, white bristles, have a dull surface and the hilum does not open into a hole or mouth.

Yellow bedstraw (Galium verum)

Seeds of yellow bedstraw are a similar reniform shape, brown colour, grooved hilum and glabrous, wrinkled surface as smooth bedstraw.

Yellow bedstraw seeds tend to be longer than smooth bedstraw by up to 0.5 mm, a more curved shape in cross-section and the surface is more wrinkled with a bubbled appearance.
