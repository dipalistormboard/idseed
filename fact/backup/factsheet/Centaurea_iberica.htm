Centaurea iberica

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Iberian starthistle

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.
  • Listed on the List of Pests Regulated by Canada established under the Plant Protection Act. D-12-01 Phytosanitary requirements to prevent the introduction of plants regulated as pests in Canada.

Distribution

Canadian: Absent from Canada (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to areas from eastern Europe to extreme western China and India. Introduced in the United States, where it has been found in the Pacific states, Wyoming and Kansas (CFIA 2012). Also introduced in Argentina and outside of its native range in Europe (CABI 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Biennial; may behave as an annual or short-lived perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 3.0 - 4.0 mm

  • Width: 1.5 mm
  • Pappus length: 1.0 - 2.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oblong with rounded bottom and flat top, compressed; large notch near the base along the narrow edge

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth with satin sheen

Seed Colour

  • White and brown-streaked; variable in colouration

Other Seed Features

  • White pappus is composed of flat bristles and variable lengths.
  • Outer bristles tend to be shortest, increasing in length towards the inside.

Habitat and Crop Association

Fields, pastures, rangelands, woodlands, shrublands, banks of watercourses, roadsides and disturbed areas (CABI 2016, DiTomaso and Healy 2007). In the United States, it is highly competitive along roadsides and in rangelands (ODA 2007).

General Information

Iberian starthistle was accidently introduced into the western United States, from where it has since spread, most notably in the 1920s (DiTomaso and Healy 2007) and the 1950s (Graham and Johnson 2003).

Similar Species

Purple starthistle (Centaurea calcitrapa)

The achenes of purple starthistle are a similar size, shape and surface with reddish-brown streaks that appear as a wood-grain.

The achenes of purple starthistle have a smaller, diamond-shaped basal notch, dull surface and lack a pappus compared to Iberian starthistle.
