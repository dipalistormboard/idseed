Centaurea stoebe

Family

Asteraceae

Synonyms

Centaurea maculosa

Common Name

Spotted knapweed

Regulation

  • Prohibited Noxious, Class 1 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act. All imported and domestic seed must be free of Prohibited Noxious weed seeds.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, NB, NS, ON, QC, YT (CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+, Darbyshire 2003).

Worldwide: Native to eastern Europe and western Asia. Introduced in other parts of Europe, as well as North America, Australia and New Zealand (CABI 2016, USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size:

  • Length: 3.0 mm

  • Width: 1.5 mm

  • Pappus length: 1.5 - 2.0 mm

Seed Shape:

  • Oblong with rounded bottom and flat top, compressed; large notch near the base

Seed Surface Texture:

  • Smooth, dull

Seed Colour:

  • Dark greenish-brown with pale longitudinal lines and a pale area around the basal notch.

Other Seed Features:

  • Collar at top of the achene is narrow and yellowish colour.

Habitat and Crop Association

Rangelands, grasslands, open sites, roadsides, riverbanks and disturbed areas. Reported as a weed of alfalfa but not usually associated with crop production (CABI 2016).

General Information

Spotted knapweed was accidentally introduced into North America in contaminated alfalfa and clover seed in the late 1800s (Ochmann 2001).

Spotted knapweed can thrive over a wide range of climate and soil conditions (CABI 2016), although it prefers well-drained, light to coarse textured soils.

Similar species: Diffuse knapweed (Centurea diffusa) achenes

Similar Species

Diffuse knapweed (Centurea diffusa)

Diffuse knapweed achenes are: a similar size, shape, pappus and dark colouring with pale stripes as spotted knapweed

Diffuse knapweed is narrower, with a strong central stripe and side stripes that fade in the middle compared to spotted knapweed, with a wider pale collar at the top of the achene. 

The achene colour of diffused knapweed is reddish-tinged while spotted knapweed is greenish-tinged. The pappus is also shorter by 1.0 mm in diffuse knapweed.
