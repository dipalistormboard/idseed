Cerastium spp.

Family

Caryophyllaceae

Common Name

Chickweed

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 4 and Noxious, Class 5 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: The genus includes 18 species found in Canada, distributed across all provinces and territories (Brouillet et al. 2010+). Of these, 13 are native to at least some provinces and the other 5 are introduced (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: The genus contains about 100 species, originating mainly from north temperate regions of Eurasia and North America and widely introduced around the world (FNA 1993+, Mabberley 2008, USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual or perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Seed Size

  • Length range: 0.8 - 1.25 mm
  • Width range: 0.5 - 1.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Ovate to wedge-shaped, tapering to hilum end; flattened

Seed Surface Texture

  • Tuberculate from stellate bases

Seed Colour

  • Reddish-brown

Other Seed Features

  • The hilum is in a notch at the narrow end of the seed.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, old fields, pastures, rangelands, meadows, gardens, lawns, turf, ditches, roadsides and disturbed areas (Frankton and Muligan 1993, Royer and Dickinson 1999, Darbyshire 2003). In Canada, not a significant problem in cultivated fields where they do not survive tillage, but can become problematic in overgrazed pastures (Royer and Dickinson 1999).

General Information

The two most commonly encountered species in Canada are field chickweed (Cerastium arvense) and mouse-eared chickweed (Cerastium fontanum subsp. vulgare). Field chickweed appears to be both native and introduced in North America, while mouse-eared chickweed is native to Eurasia (Darbyshire 2003).

Field chickweed is problematic in overgrazed rangeland and pastures (Royer and Dickinson 1999). Mouse-eared chickweed can be found in cultivated fields (Darbyshire 2003), and the seed may remain viable for up to 68 years (DiTomaso and Healy 2007).

Similar Species

Common chickweed (Stellaria media)

Common chickweed seed is a similar size, rounded shape, reddish-brown colour and tuberculate-stellate surface as chickweed.

Common chickweed seed tends to be rounder in outline with a closed notch at the hilum, an indistinct stellate pattern on the surface, and is translucent rather than opaque as in chickweed.
