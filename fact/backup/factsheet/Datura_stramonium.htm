Datura stramonium

Family

Solanaceae

Common Name

Jimsonweed

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, MB, NB, NS, ON, PE, QC, SK (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Exact native range obscure (USDA-ARS 2016), present in Asia, Africa, North and South America, Europe, Australia and New Zealand (CABI 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.5 - 3.5 mm
  • Width: 2.5 - 3.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • D-shaped, compressed, hilum is along the narrow edge

Seed Surface Texture

  • Dull, pitted surface with wrinkles

Seed Colour

  • Dull black to brown

Other Seed Features

  • The hilum is triangular, 1.0 mm long and filled with white to pinkish tissue.
  • There is a small bump at the bottom of the hilum.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, row crops, old fields, gardens, farmyards, fencelines, shores, roads and disturbed areas (Royer and Dickinson 1999, Darbyshire 2003). A weed of soybeans, beans, corn, tobacco, tomatoes and sweet peppers in the midwestern United States and southern Ontario and Quebec (Weaver and Warwick 1984).

General Information

Jimsonweed is an aggressive colonizer of agricultural fields in almost any summer crop. Plants may produce 1300-30,000 seeds, and these may survive up to 39 years in the soil (CABI 2016). Poisonous to livestock and humans (Royer and Dickinson 1999).

Similar Species

Buffalo bur (Solanum rostratum)

The seeds are a similar rounded D-shape, reticulate surface, flattened and black colour as jimsonweed.

Buffalo bur seeds have a deeply pitted surface, wavy edges, open hilum and are up to 1.0 mm smaller than jimsonweed.

Large thorn apple (Datura ferox)

The seeds of large thorn apple are the same D-shape, size, black colour and dull, pitted and wrinkled surface as jimsonweed.

The hilum of large thorn apple is oval with rounded edges, not triangular with straight edges as jimsonweed.  There is also a notch next to the hilum, rather than a bump as in jimsonweed.
