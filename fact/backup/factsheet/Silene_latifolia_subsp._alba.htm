Silene latifolia subsp. alba

Family

Caryophyllaceae

Synonyms

Silene pratensis

Common Name

White cockle

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB,BC, MB, NB, NL, NS, ON, PE, QC and SK (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, parts of temperate Asia and Europe (USDA-ARS 2016). Naturalized in other parts of temperate Asia, Australia, New Zealand, North America and southern South America (USDA-ARS 2016). Abundant in the northern United States, particularly east of the Dakotas, infesting 30% of the legume acreage in some areas (McNeill 1977).

Duration of Life Cycle

Biennial to short-lived perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 1.3 - 1.5mm
  • Width: 0.9 - 1.3mm

Seed Shape

  • Inflated oval

Seed Surface Texture

  • Surface is densely covered with tubercles, arranged in rows along the margins, but appear random on the side of the seed.

Seed Colour

  • Greyish-brown to bluish-grey
  • Immature seeds are reddish

Other Features

  • A prominent collar extends one-half to two-thirds around the hilum, forming a distinctive 'open mouth'.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields (especially legume forage crops and small grains), gardens, pastures, orchards, fencerows, old fields, railway lines, roadsides and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, McNeill 1977).

General Information

White cockle prefers full sun and rich, well-drained soils (McNeill 1977). This species reproduces mainly by seed, producing up 24,000 seeds per plant, but can also propagate from root fragments (McNeill 1977).

Similar Species

Night-flowering catchfly (Silene noctiflora)

Similar shape, size, pads near hilum as white cockle.

White cockle seed is usually covered in a whitish bloom, has an 'open mouth' hilum, with a round, inflated shape. Night-flowering catchfly seed may have a yellowish bloom, or is reddish in colour. The hilum is bordered by two small pads and the seed does not appear inflated in profile.

Bladder campion (Silene vulgaris)

Similar shape, size, pads near hilum as white cockle.

White cockle seed is usually covered in a whitish bloom, has an 'open mouth' hilum, with a round, inflated shape. Bladder campion seed is usually greyish. The hilum is bordered by two large pads, with surface tubercles in rows and is angular in profile.
