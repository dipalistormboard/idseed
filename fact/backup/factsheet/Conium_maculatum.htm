Conium maculatum

Family

Apiaceae

Common Name

Poison hemlock

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, BC, NB, NS, ON, QC, SK (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to northern Africa, Europe and western and central Asia. Introduced to North and South America, southern Africa, Australia, New Zealand and Micronesia (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Biennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Mericarp

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.0 - 4.0 mm
  • Width: 1.5 - 2.5 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval to teardrop-shaped, flat on one side

Seed Surface Texture

  • Dull surface, wrinkled between the ribs

Seed Colour

  • Greyish-brown to brown with straw-yellow ribs

Other Seed Features

  • Musty smell if seed surface is punctured.
  • Five ribs on the rounded side; no ribs on the flat side of the mericarp.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, pastures, roads and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, CABI 2016). Invades a variety crops, including cereal and vegetable crops, as well as orchards, often by encroachment from field edges (CABI 2016).

General Information

Poison hemlock was brought to the United States as a garden plant in the 19th century (DiTomaso and Healy 2007). Seed may have spread by mud, boots and other human clothing, as well as by machinery and transported soil (Ministry of Agriculture, Food, and Fisheries 2002).

Poison hemlock grows in lowlands on dry to moist soils and can tolerate poorly drained soils (Ministry of Agriculture, Food, and Fisheries 2002). It is poisonous to livestock (CABI 2016).

Similar Species

Wild carrot (Daucus carota subsp. carota)

The fruits of wild carrot and poison hemlock are a similar size, teardrop shape and grey-brown colour.

Wild carrot fruits may lack the typical long wings and hairs if processed, immature or damaged.

Wild carrot fruits may still retain small hairs on the straight ribs, the surface between the ribs is smooth, and thin ribs are seen on the flat side.  Poison hemlock does not have hairs, ribs are wavy and the surface is wrinkled.  The flat side of the fruit does not have ribs.
