Ambrosia trifida

Family

Asteraceae

Common Name

Giant ragweed

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in AB, MB, NB, NS, ON, PE, QC and SK. Reported as ephemeral in BC (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Native to North America and introduced to Asia (China, Georgia, Israel, Japan) and Europe (USDA-ARS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Annual

Seed/Fruit Type

Achene within a bur

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Bur length: 4.0-7.0 mm
  • Bur width: 4.0 -6.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Burs are oval with a narrow base and a large spine at the top

Seed Surface Texture

  • Woody and dull

Seed Colour

  • Dull greyish-brown, yellowish-brown or brown

Other Seed Features

  • A ring of 5 or 6 small spines is found near the top of the seed.
  • Thin ribs extend down the sides of the fruit.

Habitat and Crop Association

Old fields, field margins, pastures, gardens, fencerows, shores, ditches, roadsides, railway lines, and disturbed areas (Darbyshire 2003, CABI 2016). A weed of many broadleaved crops (CABI 2016).

General Information

Dispersal of giant ragweed can occur through agricultural transport or roadside mowing (Von der Lippe et al. 2013) and it can also be a contaminant of bird and livestock feed (European Food Safety Authority 2010).

Giant ragweed is an aggressive weed of grain crops and no-tillage fields (Regnier et al. 2008). It is a major cause of hay fever (CABI 2016).

Similar species: Common ragweed (Ambrosia artemisiifolia) achenes

Similar Species

Common ragweed (Ambrosia artemisiifolia)

Common ragweed burs are a similar ovate shape and a similar arrangement of spines as giant ragweed.

Common ragweed burs are up to 9.0 mm shorter, and have smaller spines than giant ragweed.
