Pastinaca sativa

Family

Apiaceae

Common Name

Wild parsnip

Regulation

  • Secondary Noxious, Class 3 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian: Occurs in all Canadian provinces and territories except NU (Brouillet et al. 2010+).

Worldwide: Native to Europe and western temperate Asia (USDA-ARS 2016). Naturalized in southern Africa, eastern Asia, Australia, New Zealand, North America and parts of South America (USDA-ARS 2016). It is widespread in the United States, except in the southeast (USDA-NRCS 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Biennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Mericarp

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 3.5 - 4.5 mm
  • Width: 4.5 - 6.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Oval-shaped, flattened

Seed Surface Texture

  • Smooth, outer side is roughened

Seed Colour

  • Straw-yellow with reddish oil ducts

Other Seed Features

  • The outer side has four oil ducts that extend almost the length of the fruit; the inner side has three.
  • The outer side has three thin, arched ribs between the oil ducts, the inner side has none.

Habitat and Crop Association

Pastures, gardens, old fields, riverbanks, railway embankments, roadsides, field edges and disturbed areas (Cain et al. 2010, Darbyshire 2003).

General Information

Wild parsnip is believed to be naturalized from cultivation and possibly from seed contamination (Cain et al. 2010). Cultivated parsnip was first documented in Canada in 1612 and by the late-1700s, this species was found established in a wild state around historical European settlements in eastern Canada (Cain et al. 2010).

Similar species: Cow parsnip (Heracleum maximum) mericarps

Similar species: Cow parsnip (Heracleum maximum) mericarp

Similar Species

Cow parsnip (Heracleum maximum)

The fruits of cow parsnip are a flat oval shape, straw yellow colour with elongated reddish oil ducts when compared to wild parsnip.

Wild parsnip fruits can be up to half as long as cow parsnip. Cow parsnip fruits do not have three ribs on the outer side of the fruit like wild parsnip.
