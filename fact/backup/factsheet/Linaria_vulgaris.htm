Linaria vulgaris

Family

Plantaginaceae

Common Name

Yellow toadflax

Regulation

  • Primary Noxious, Class 2 in the Canadian Weed Seeds Order (2016) under the Seeds Act.

Distribution

Canadian:Occurs across Canada except in NU (Brouillet et al. 2010+, CFIA and NRCan/CFS 2011+).

Worldwide: Native to Europe and temperate Asia (USDA-ARS 2016) and introduced in North America, Chile, South Africa, Australia and New Zealand (CABI 2016).

Duration of Life Cycle

Perennial

Seed/Fruit Type

Seed

Seed Identification Features

Average Seed Size

  • Length: 2.5 mm
  • Width: 2.0 mm

Seed Shape

  • Round to oval with a wing around the outside; strongly flattened

Seed Surface Texture

  • The centre of the seed is covered with tubercles

Seed Colour

  • Black with bronze highlights

Other Seed Features

  • A thin wing surrounds the seed, 0.3-0.5 mm wide, minutely reticulated.

Habitat and Crop Association

Cultivated fields, old fields, pastures, rangelands, shores, roadsides and disturbed areas (Saner et al. 1995, Darbyshire 2003). In Canada, it is a problem in pastures and crops such as wheat, barley, oats, canola and peas (Saner et al. 1995, CABI 2016).

General Information

Yellow toadflax was once grown as an ornamental, dye and medicinal plant (Saner et al. 1995). This species first appeared in Ontario and Quebec at the end of the 19th century and moved into the Prairie Provinces after 1920, through contaminated alfalfa seed and as a garden plant (Saner et al. 1995). Yellow toadflax is a prolific seed producer and reproduces from both seed and clonal propagation (Saner et al. 1995).

Similar Species

Melancholy toadflax (Linaria tristis)

Both species have rounded, flattened seeds that are a similar size and colour to common toadflax.

Melancholy toadflax may be smooth or have a few tubercles like common toadflax, but only on one side of the seed.
